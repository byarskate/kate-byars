Envie App

Envie is a financial budgeting app that I created as a part of the CareerFoundry Product Design Certification course. One of my hobbies is personal finance and I am a busy parent so I personally a need quick, visual solution when it comes to budgeting so I created an envelope banking app that helps the user know quickly what they have available to spend.

SWOT, UX Analysis, Strategy & Marketing Profile

Interviews

Two repeated concerned surfaced in my interviews:

  • Participants without a spouse or life partner wanted an app that would serve as their financial confidante and advisor. That is, they wanted the app to make sense of their past behavior and recommend new ways to save and spend.
  • Married and partnered participants requested a way to get on the same page with their partner about financial decisions.

Meet Elly and Grayton

I created 2 personas for each of my user segments based on interviews. This helped me to keep an objective eye on the app's development. Elly is a professional mom who acts as the primary family financial manager.

Grayton is an aspiring actor with variable income. He frequently splits expenses with friends.

Customer Journey & Task Analysis

I created journey maps for my two primary personas to help me unearth potential gaps in my thinking. Elly wants to save automatically for big goals, so I wanted to discover if there was a way to help her. Grayton wants to get a better handle on his budgeting and saving. I examined both of these pain points in Journey Maps. For Elly, I added an automatic transfer option within the envelopes and for Grayton, I added a Coach area which creates personalized advice based on quizzes and past spending behavior.

Information Architecture

Card Sort, Site Map, Content Audit

To determine the final site map of the app, I first conducted a content audit of a competitor site and an affinity map. Then, to test the validity of my ideas, I conducted an online card sort with 6 participants. I drew up an initial plan for the app's site map. I hope to gain insight into my user's mental models via the card sort. Several key areas surfaced among the users:

  • A group of features that were already intuitive and delightful to use.
  • A group of features that were confusing or which users thought performed a function different from what was intended.

From this list, I tweaked my site map and created a small list of features to revise.

Sketches & Wireframes

I started by creating paper wireframes and moved to mid fidelity prototypes. I created three revisions of the high fidelity prototypes to create an accessible app that flows well with the user's intuition.Using Figma, I translated my low fidelity sketches into mid-fidelity wireframes and a prototype. Then, I improved them by refining the flows and thinking more about how to reduce user friction and confusion.

Usability Testing

I then created a high fidelity prototype in Figma. I recruited and tested 6 participants in a Usability Test. Four issues surfaced as the most frequent and critical:

  • 67% Splash screens not always clickable
  • 83% Not all sign up inputs are fillable
  • 33% Date Picker not selectable
  • 17% Bottom Navigation labels too small to read

A/B Testing

I tested images for the onboarding process because this is the screen that will cause them to proceed to Sign Up. Text copy and size were kept the same but color schemes were different due to readability issues.

UI Design

Once the usability issues were resolved, I moved on to finalize the UI design in Figma. Originally, I chose a pastel color scheme but upon further research into Color Psychology and upon checking with the competition, I opted instead for a deep blue which is associated with trust, loyalty and wisdom since these themes surfaced in my interviews. I chose white as a high contrast accent color. I designed for Android using the Material 3 Design kit.
